DanceChat: Natural Language-Guided Music-to-Dance Generation

Anonymous
Paper Supplementary Code arXiv

Abstract

Music-to-dance generation aims to create human dance movements precisely structured to input music. Recent advancements have shown promising results in generating dance from music, yet significant challenges remain due to the semantic gap between music and dance. Unlike text, which can directly describe physical motion, music lacks explicit information about body movements, making it difficult to achieve both musical alignment and stylistic diversity in generated dance sequences. In this paper, we introduce DanceChat, a natural language-guided music-to-dance generation model inspired by the real-world process of dance learning, where learners rely on choreographers for guidance. In DanceChat, We treat our model as a dance learner, and utilize a Large Language Model (LLM) to perform the role of a choreographer to guide the dance learner with precise instructions. In this way, we inject dance motion instruction which can guide the model to learn dancing with more diversity and alignment with the style of the music. Our approach consists of three core components: (1) an LLM-based guide that generates dance movement instructions based on music structure and style, (2) a multimodal fusion paradigm that enriches music features with both beat-aware and textual information, and (3) a multimodal alignment loss that bridges the semantic gap between music and motion, enhancing rhythmic and stylistic coherence. Extensive experiments on the AIST++ dataset, along with human evaluations, demonstrate that DanceChat outperforms state-of-the-art methods qualitatively and quantitatively.

Method

DanceChat integrates three components: (a) a Pseudo Instruction Generation module that extracts musical features like tempo and key, (b) a Multi-modal Fusion module that unifies music, beat, and text representations, and (c) a Diffusion-based Motion Synthesis module that generates realistic dance movements.
